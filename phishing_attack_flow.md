# Phishing Attack Flow Analysis

## Overview
Phishing is one of the most common cyber fraud techniques used to steal sensitive information such as login credentials, banking details, and personal data.

This document explains a typical phishing attack lifecycle from attacker preparation to victim impact.

---

## Attack Flow Stages

### 1. Attacker Preparation
- Creation of fake websites or login pages
- Spoofed email IDs or phone numbers
- Social engineering message drafting

### 2. Delivery Method
- Email phishing
- SMS phishing (Smishing)
- Social media messages

### 3. Victim Interaction
- Victim clicks malicious link
- Victim enters sensitive information
- Data is transmitted to attacker-controlled servers

### 4. Exploitation
- Unauthorized account access
- Financial fraud
- Identity misuse

### 5. Detection & Reporting
- Victim notices suspicious activity
- Complaint registered to cyber crime portal or cyber cell
- Initial verification by authorities

---

## Key Observations
- Human error is the primary vulnerability
- Urgency and fear are common psychological triggers
- Early detection reduces damage

---

## Educational Note
This analysis is based on cybersecurity learning and general fraud patterns. No real case data is included.
